### THE SMART STALL

SMART STALL: The Duchampian Master/Slave

Telecommunications Interface

THE SMART STALL was exhibited at The Bridge-- the 1996

SIGGRAPH Art Show in New Orleans. The SMART STALL is

conceived as an immersive interactive theatrical experience,

having a well defined beginning, middle and end. Taking

inspiration from Antonin Artauds Theatre of Cruelty the

users motion triggers orders and instructions from an

abusive semi-intelligent agent suffering from multiple

personality disorder.

In homage to Duchamps avatar  - R. Mutt - this installation

looks like a public toilet stall but actually is a

telecommunications terminal. Non functioning bathroom toilet

stalls were constructed at the Conference Center and the

Contemporary Art Center in New Orleans. Each was equipped

with a digital white board which digitized handwritten

graffiti and transmitted it via a T3 line to the remote stall

where the message was projected. Infrared motion detectors

and magnet switches trigger audible instructions.

Like the Automatic Confession Machine, THE SMART STALL,

while making use of technology questions our infatuation with

it. THE SMART STALL challenges the aims of ubiquitous

computing. It calls into question the authority of technology by

the medium of immersive theatrical form where participation

in this virtual stage requires the willing suspension of

disbelief as the compliant user is ordered about by a virtual

authoritarian agent. As Brenda Laurel has noted the potential

of computers is not in its ability to perform calculations but

in its capacity to represent action in which humans could

participate.
