Professor "P" as he is called in computer graphic circles is one of the foremost researchers in virtual models of morphogensis (the form and structure of animals and plants - more exactly: the formation and differentiation of tissues and organs). The Professor has been instrumental in furthering the implementations of Lindenmayer-system modelers (L-Systems). Inspired by the software that Professor Prusinkiewicz had developed I initiated a research project with the assistance of The Banff Centre for the Arts. This research carried out with the crucial programming assistance of* [*Tom Malaher*](http://www.netstart.com/tmalaher) *integrates L-Systems within a VRML environment. The initial model, a plant growing in the 3d VRML network environment, was built in early 1998.*  *The intent here was to demonstrate L-Systems in a VRML environment and propose the possibility of creating an ever changing environment in virtual reality. L-Systems could and can be used to create extensive, complex, dynamic environments in virtual space. They could and can be used to build the worlds themselves - to not only provide the walls, floors, and plans of the polygon environment - but elements within that environment - 3dL-Systems seemed an ideal way of creating an every changing, interactive, environment.*  *I proposed to develop the potential of this integration in a DVD and online site that would take as its subject matter the issue of the architectural, nature, and the human. The work would apply models of morphogenesis to a virtual architectural environment that users would travel through, change as they willed (or allow for other sorts of parameters to change the environment -time, chance, etc...), viewing content - video documentary sequences evocative of the question.
