## Global Refusal Manifesto

Article by Paul-Émile Borduas

## Global Refusal Manifesto

Offspring of modest French-Canadian families, working class or lower middle class, from the arrival of the country to the present day, remaining French and Catholic through resistance to victory, through attachment, arbitrary to the past, through pleasure and sentimental pride and other necessities.

A colony plunged into the smooth walls of fear as early as 1760, the usual refuge of the vanquished; there, abandoned for the first time. The elite returned to the sea or sold themselves to the highest bidder. They would not fail to do so whenever the opportunity arose.

A small people tightly bound to the cassocks, which remained the sole custodians of faith, knowledge, truth, and national wealth. Kept apart from the universal evolution of thought, fraught with risks and dangers, educated without ill will but without oversight, in the misinterpretation of great historical events when complete ignorance was impractical.

A small people descended from a Jansenist colony, isolated, defeated, and defenseless against the invasion of all the religious orders of France and Navarre, desperate to perpetuate in these blessed places of fear (this is the beginning of wisdom!) the prestige and benefits of Catholicism, so beleaguered in Europe. Heirs to papal authority, mechanical and unassailable, grand masters of obscurantist methods, our educational institutions thus have the means to organize, as a monopoly, the reign of exploitative memory, of stagnant reason, of malevolent intent.

A small people who, despite everything, multiply in the generosity of the flesh if not in that of the spirit, in the north of immense America with the vibrant body of youth with a heart of gold, but with a simian morality, bewitched by the annihilating prestige of the memory of the masterpieces of Europe, disdainful of the authentic creations of its oppressed classes.

Our fate seemed hard-won.

However, revolutions and external wars break the impermeability of the charm, the effectiveness of the spiritual blockade.

Uncontrollable beads of sweat ooze from the walls.

Political struggles become fiercely partisan. The clergy, against all hope, commits imprudent acts.

Revolts ensued, followed by a few capital executions. The first passionate rifts emerged between the clergy and some of the faithful.

Slowly the breach widens, narrows, widens again.

Foreign travel is on the rise. Paris exerts a powerful pull. Too vast in time and space, too restless for our timid souls, it often provides little more than a vacation spent perfecting a belated sex education and acquiring, through a stay in France, the easy authority necessary for the improved exploitation of the masses upon our return. With very few exceptions, our doctors, for example (whether they have traveled or not), adopt scandalous behavior (one must, after all, pay for those long years of study!).

Revolutionary works, when they happen to fall into our hands, appear to be the bitter fruits of a group of eccentrics. Academic activity has a different prestige, one that stems from our lack of judgment.

These journeys also offer, among other things, an exceptional opportunity for awakening. The unattainable seeps in everywhere. Forbidden readings spread. They bring a little balm and hope.

Consciences are awakened by the invigorating influence of the cursed poets: these men who, without being monsters, dare to express aloud what the most unfortunate among us stifle in silence, consumed by self-loathing and the terror of being swallowed alive. A glimmer of light shines through, exemplified by these men who are the first to embrace the anxieties of our time, so painful, so lost. The answers they offer possess a different kind of unsettling quality, precision, and freshness than the endless refrains offered in Quebec and in seminaries around the globe.

The boundaries of our dreams are no longer the same.

We are overcome with dizziness as the tattered remnants of once-bursting horizons fall away. The shame of hopeless servitude gives way to the pride of a possible freedom, to be won through hard-fought struggle.

To hell with the bottle brush and the toque!

A thousand times they extorted what they had once given.

Beyond Christianity we touch the burning human fraternity of which it has become the closed door.

The reign of multifaceted fear is over.

In the vain hope of erasing the memory, I list them: fear of prejudice - fear of public opinion - of persecution - of general disapproval fear of being alone without God and a society that very inevitably isolates fear of oneself - of one's brother - of poverty fear of the established order - of ridiculous justice fear of new relationships fear of the superrational fear of necessities fear of the floodgates wide open to faith in humanity - in future society fear of all forms that might trigger a transformative love Blue fear - red fear - white fear: link in our chain.

From the reign of subtractive fear we move to that of anguish.

One would have had to be made of iron to remain indifferent to the pain of biases masking feigned gaiety, of the psychological reflexes of the most cruel extravagances: the cellophanewrapped garment of poignant, ever-present despair (how could one not cry out upon reading the news of this horrific collection of lampshades made from tattoos taken from unfortunate captives at the behest of an elegant woman; not groan at the interminable enumeration of the tortures of the concentration camps; not feel a chill run down one's spine at the description of the Spanish dungeons, the unjustifiable reprisals, the cold-blooded revenge). How could one not shudder before the cruel lucidity of science?

This reign of all-powerful anguish is followed by that of nausea.

We were disgusted by humanity's apparent inability to correct evils, by the futility of our efforts, by the vanity of our past hopes.

For centuries the generous objects of poetic activity have been doomed to fatal failure on the social level, violently rejected from the frameworks of society with subsequent attempts to use them in the irrevocable distortion of integration, of false assimilation.

For centuries, splendid revolutions, brimming with lifeblood, have been crushed to death after a brief moment of delirious hope, in the barely interrupted slide of the irremediable descent:

the French revolutions the Russian Revolution

the Spanish revolution thwarted in an international melee despite the helpless wishes of so many simple souls around the world.

Once again, fate proved stronger than generosity.

Not to feel nauseous at the rewards given to gross cruelties, liars, forgers, makers of stillborn objects, refiners, self-interested, calculating, false guides of humanity, poisoners of living sources.

Not to feel nauseous at our own cowardice, our powerlessness, our fragility, our incomprehension.

Faced with the disasters of our love...

In contrast to the constant preference given to cherished illusions over objective mysteries.

Where is the secret of this efficiency of misfortune imposed on man and by man alone, if not in our relentless pursuit of the civilization that presides over the destinies of dominant nations?

The United States, Russia, England, France, Germany, Italy and Spain: sharp-toothed heirs of a single decalogue, of the same gospel.

The religion of Christ dominated the universe. You see what we did with it: sometimes sisters have become sister farms.

Remove the specific forces of competition over raw materials, prestige, and authority, and they will be in perfect agreement. Give supremacy to whomever you wish, complete control to whomever you please, and you will have the same fundamental results, if not with the same adjustments to the details.

All of them represent the culmination of Christian civilization.

The next world war will see its collapse through the elimination of opportunities for international competition.

Its cadaverous state will be striking even with eyes still closed.

The decomposition that began in the 14th century will make even the least sensitive feel nauseous.

Its execrable exploitation, maintained for so many centuries in efficiency at the cost of the most precious qualities of life, will finally be revealed to the multitude of its victims: docile slaves all the more determined to defend it because they were more miserable.

The ordeal will come to an end.

Christian decadence will have dragged down with it all the peoples, all the classes that it has touched, in order from the first to the last, from top to bottom.

It will reach, in shame, the inverted equivalent of the peaks of the 13th century.

By the 13th century, the limits of moral development and the all-encompassing relationships of the early period had been reached, and intuition gave way to reason. Gradually, the act of faith was replaced by a calculated one. Exploitation within religion began with the self-serving manipulation of existing, entrenched sentiments; with the rational study of sacred texts to maintain the supremacy that had been spontaneously attained.

Rational exploitation slowly extends to all social activities: maximum efficiency is required.

Faith takes refuge in the heart of the crowd, becoming the last hope for revenge, the ultimate compensation. But there too, hopes are fading.

At the highest levels, mathematics has replaced metaphysical speculations, which have become futile.

The spirit of observation succeeds that of transfiguration.

The method introduces imminent progress into the limited. Decadence becomes agreeable and necessary: it fosters the birth of our supple machines with their dizzying movement, it allows us to straitjacket our tumultuous rivers while awaiting the planet's deliberate disintegration. Our scientific instruments give us extraordinary means of investigation, of controlling what is too small, too fast, too vibrant, too slow, or too large for us. Our reason allows for the invasion of the world, but one in which we have lost our unity.

The divide between psychic powers and reasoning powers is nearing its peak.

Material progress, reserved for the propertied classes, methodically held back, allowed political evolution with the help of religious powers (later without them) but without renewing the foundations of our sensibility, of our subconscious, without allowing the full emotional evolution of the crowd which alone could have pulled us out of the deep Christian rut.

The society born in faith will perish by the weapon of reason: intention.

The fatal regression of collective moral power into strictly individual and sentimental power has woven the lining of the already prestigious screen of abstract knowledge under which society hides to comfortably devour the fruits of its misdeeds.

The last two wars were necessary to bring about this absurd state of affairs. The horror of the third will be decisive. The hour of total sacrifice is drawing near.

Already the European rats are attempting a desperate escape across the Atlantic. Events will sweep over the voracious, the sated, the luxurious, the calm, the blind, and the deaf.

They will be mercilessly overthrown.

A new collective hope will be born.

Already it demands the fervor of exceptional lucidities, the anonymous union in the rediscovered faith in the future, in the future collectivity.

The magical booty, magically conquered from the unknown, awaits its turn. It was gathered by all the true poets. Its transformative power is measured by the violence exerted against it, by its subsequent resistance to attempts at use (after more than two centuries, Sade remains unavailable in bookstores; Isidore Ducasse, for more than a century since his death, of revolutions, of carnage, despite the habit of the current cesspool, remains too virile for the soft consciences of our time).

All the objects in the treasure prove inviolable to our society. They remain the incorruptible, sensitive reserve of tomorrow. They were spontaneously ordered outside and against civilization. They await the fulfillment of current needs before becoming active (on a social level).

Until then, our duty is simple.

To break definitively with all the habits of society, to disassociate ourselves from its utilitarian spirit. A refusal to knowingly fall short of our psychic and physical capabilities. A refusal to turn a blind eye to the vices and deceptions perpetrated under the guise of knowledge, service rendered, and due recognition. A refusal to be confined to the mere plastic village, a fortified place but easily avoided. A refusal to remain silent-do with us what you will, but you must listen to us-a refusal of glory and honors (the former granted): stigmas of harm, unconsciousness, and servility. A refusal to serve, to be used for such ends. A refusal of all INTENTION, the nefarious weapon of REASON. Down with both, relegated to the sidelines!

Let the magic begin!

Let's get to the objective mysteries!

Make way for love!

Let's get down to basics!

To the blanket refusal we oppose full responsibility.

Self-interested action remains attached to its author; it is stillborn.

Passionate acts elude us because of their own dynamism.

We gladly take full responsibility for tomorrow. Rational effort, once we look back, is tasked with freeing the present from the limbo of the past.

Our passions spontaneously, unpredictably, necessarily shape the future.

The past must be accepted along with birth; it cannot be sacred. We are always even with it.

It is naive and unhealthy to view the men and events of history through the amplifying lens of fame, which attributes to them qualities inaccessible to present-day humanity. Certainly, these qualities are beyond the grasp of skillful academic mimicry, but they are automatically attainable whenever a person obeys the deepest needs of their being; whenever a person consents to be a new person in a new era. This is the definition of every person, for every era.

No more mass assassination of the present and the future by repeated blows from the past.

We simply need to identify today's needs with those of yesterday. At best, tomorrow will be nothing more than the unpredictable consequence of the present.

We don't have to worry about it before it happens.

## FINAL SETTLEMENT OF ACCOUNTS

The organized forces of society reproach us for our zeal for work, the overflow of our worries,

our excesses as an insult to their softness, their tranquility, their good taste for life (generous, full of hope and love by lost habit).

The regime's friends suspect us of supporting the "Revolution." The "Revolution's" friends transform it, not to change it."

suspect us of being nothing but rebels: "...we protest against what is, but with the sole desire to However delicately it may be said, we believe we understand. This is about class. We are accused of the naive intention of wanting to "transform" society by replacing men in power with others like them. So, why not them, of course! But they're not from the same class! As if changing classes implied changing civilizations, changing desires, changing hopes! They dedicate themselves, on a fixed salary plus a cost-of-living allowance, to organizing the proletariat; they are absolutely right. The problem is that once victory is firmly established, in addition to their current meager wages, they will demand, always and in the same way, from the same proletariat, additional payments and long-term renewals, without any possibility of discussion. We do, however, acknowledge that they are part of the historical pattern. Salvation can only come after the greatest excess of exploitation. They will be this excess. They will be, inevitably, without the need for anyone in particular. The feast will be sumptuous. We have already refused to share in it. This is our "culpable abstention". To you the rationally ordered feeding frenzy (like everything that is within the affectionate embrace of decadence); to us the unpredictable passion; to us the total risk in the global refusal.

(It is beyond the realm of possibility that social classes have succeeded one another in governing peoples without being able to do anything other than perpetuate the irrevocable decadence. It is beyond the realm of possibility that our historical knowledge assures us that only a complete development of our faculties first, and then a perfect renewal of emotional sources, can extricate us from this impasse and set us on the path to a civilization eager to be born).

Everyone, those in power, those aspiring to power, are happy to spoil us, if only we would agree to limit their opportunities for distortion by carefully balancing our activities.

Fortune is ours if we lower our visors, plug our ears, pull up our boots and boldly forge our way through the crowd, left and right.

We prefer to be cynical spontaneously, without malice.

Kind people smile at the lack of monetary success of our group exhibitions, thus they have the charming impression of being the first to discover their small market value.

If we hold exhibition after exhibition, it's not in the naive hope of making a fortune. We know those who possess wealth are located on the other side of the world from where we are. They could not risk these inflammatory contacts with impunity.

In the past, unintentional misunderstandings alone have allowed such sales.

We believe this text is capable of dispelling all those of the future.

If our activities become urgent, it is because we feel intensely the urgent need for unity.

There, success explodes!

Yesterday, we were alone and undecided.

Today a group exists with deep and courageous ramifications; they already extend beyond borders.

We also have a magnificent duty: to preserve the precious treasure that has been entrusted to us. It too is part of history.

Tangible objects, they require a constantly renewed, confronted, and questioned relationship. An intangible, demanding relationship that requires the vital forces of action.

This treasure is the poetic reservoir, the emotional renewal from which future centuries will draw. It can only be transmitted TRANSFORMED, otherwise it is distorted.

Let those tempted by adventure join us.

In the imaginable future, we glimpse man freed from his useless chains, realizing in the unforeseen, necessary order of spontaneity, in resplendent anarchy, the fullness of his individual gifts.

Until then, without rest or pause, in community of feeling with those thirsting for a better life, without fear of long deadlines, in encouragement or persecution, we will joyfully pursue our wild need for liberation.
